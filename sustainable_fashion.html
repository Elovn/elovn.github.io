TO EXPERIMENT

"Being an artist you have to find a new way to express yourself and to encourage individuals to have more imagination" (Weiwei, 2009).

The constant search for belonging has turned clothing into a way of defining the personality. However, the passive behaviour derived from the consumerism and the lack of original ideologies has turned fashion into a way of labeling social status and a lifestyle imposed by the interest of the industry.

The textile industry has generated a vast financial capital, most of the times at the expense of the quality of its clothing and the preservation of natural resources, leading to damages to the environment. The lack of scientific research implies a delay in the usage of new sustainable methods of production and the lack of innovative textiles.

Ambiguity

Even a piece of trash wants to be something, again.

As Luis Kahn said "Even a brick wants to be something", raw materials are often underestimated for its own true characteristics and properties. A common example is PET, produced as a disposable container when it has a durability of 500 years on average. Here is where we find the ambiguity of plastic. In some garments in the collection I used an application of sequins through embroidery, increasing the value of the garment and even turning it into a luxury item.
